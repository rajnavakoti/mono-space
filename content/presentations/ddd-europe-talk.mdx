In strategic DDD terms, those three bypass-services are in a Conformist relationship with Consignee — they conform to its internal table schema instead of its published API, because the API didn't exist when they needed the data, or it was too slow, or someone said 'just read the table' and it became permanent. The API boundary isn't a total facade — it's a partial one. Three of five consumers treat it as if it weren't there. Three Conformists is what facade means here. The database tells a different story. This is why you need multiple lenses — one artifact alone can mislead you."
